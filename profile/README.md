## Executive Summary
**Sentry Point Cyber Intelligence** is an independent cyber research and threat intelligence enterprise specializing in advanced infrastructure auditing, defensive security operations, and adversary landscape analysis. 

Our mission is to proactively identify, document, and neutralize emerging digital threats through comprehensive analytical mapping, system log auditing, and the development of proprietary defensive automation tools to secure modern network infrastructures.

---

## Strategic Capabilities & Core Operations

* 📡 **Threat Intelligence & Reconnaissance:** Conducting deep-dive investigations into active cyber threat groups, mapping malicious command-and-control (C2) nodes, and tracking digital footprints of threat actors using advanced OSINT methodologies.
* 🛡️ **Defensive Operations & Log Analysis:** Designing advanced defense-in-depth strategies, analyzing complex system logs, monitoring network traffic anomalies, and deploying intrusion detection environments.
* 🌐 **Infrastructure & Network Engineering:** Auditing public-facing web environments, hardening Linux server environments, identifying systemic network misconfigurations, and researching attack surfaces across critical nodes.
* 🤖 **DevOps & Defensive Automation:** Architecting proprietary scripts, parsing utilities, and secure automation bots for continuous data breach monitoring, tactical intelligence aggregation, and infrastructure log auditing.
* 💻 **Secure Software Development:** Engineering resilient applications, data mining solutions, and backend tools tailored for automated security assessments and cryptographic research.

## Compliance, Ethics & Disclaimer
*Sentry Point Cyber Intelligence operates strictly within the boundaries of applicable international legal frameworks and Ukrainian cyber regulations. All activities are non-intrusive, utilizing exclusively Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT), legal network analysis, and publicly accessible telemetry. The enterprise does not engage in offensive cyber operations, unauthorized network intrusions, or the dissemination of Personally Identifiable Information (PII). All published material serves solely educational and defensive purposes to harden global infrastructure.*
